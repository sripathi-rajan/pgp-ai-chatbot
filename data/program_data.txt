1. Programme Overview

The PGP in Applied AI & Agentic Systems is a 15-month intensive postgraduate programme designed to train engineers and technology professionals to design, build, and deploy real-world Artificial Intelligence systems and autonomous agents.

Unlike traditional AI programmes that focus mainly on theoretical concepts or isolated tools, this programme emphasizes system-level thinking and practical implementation. Students learn how to integrate multiple AI technologies including:

Machine Learning

Automation systems

Data Engineering

AI Governance

AI system architecture

The programme aims to develop professionals capable of building reliable, production-ready AI systems that solve real industry problems.

A key characteristic of the programme is its hands-on approach. Students build one AI system every term, resulting in six complete AI systems over six academic terms.

This structure ensures that learners graduate with practical experience and a portfolio of AI projects rather than only theoretical knowledge.

1. Programme Overview

The PGP in Applied AI & Agentic Systems is a 15-month postgraduate professional programme designed to train individuals in building real-world Artificial Intelligence systems and autonomous agents.

The programme focuses on applied intelligence, where participants learn how to design, build, deploy, and maintain production-grade AI systems used in industry.

Unlike conventional AI courses that emphasize theoretical concepts or isolated tools, this programme emphasizes system-level thinking and practical implementation.

Students learn to integrate multiple components of modern AI systems including:

Machine Learning models

Data engineering pipelines

Automation systems

AI agents and autonomous workflows

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems

AI governance and responsible AI practices

The objective of the programme is to develop professionals capable of delivering reliable and scalable AI solutions for real-world applications.

2. Programme Duration

The programme duration is 15 months.

During this time students complete:

6 academic terms

6 AI system projects

A combination of in-class learning and out-class applied projects

Each academic term focuses on a specific stage of AI system development.

The programme is built around an applied, project-centric learning model.

7. Curriculum Structure

The curriculum is divided into two complementary tracks:

In-class Curriculum

Out-class Curriculum

The in-class curriculum focuses on theoretical understanding and technical development.

The out-class curriculum focuses on practical implementation through projects.

8. In-Class Curriculum

The in-class curriculum is organized across six academic terms.

Term 1: Data Analytics and AI Foundations

Students learn the foundational skills required to work with data and AI systems.

Topics include:

Statistics

SQL

Data visualization

Prompt engineering

Data exploration techniques

Students develop their first functional AI chatbot.

Term 2: Agents and Automation with Modern AI

This term focuses on the architecture of AI agents and automation systems.

Topics include:

AI agent design

API integration

Workflow automation

Classical machine learning methods

Students learn how AI systems can automate business operations.

Term 3: Applied Machine Learning for Real Decisions

Students build machine learning systems used for decision-making.

Topics include:

Model training

Model evaluation

Model optimization

Model deployment

Communicating insights to stakeholders

Machine learning models are applied to business strategy and operational decision-making.

Term 4: Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), Neural Networks and Data Engineering

Students learn advanced AI architecture and data infrastructure.

Topics include:

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

Neural network fundamentals

Data pipeline engineering

AI infrastructure design

Students build scalable AI assistants capable of retrieving knowledge from private data.

Term 5: Domain AI and Enterprise Machine Learning Design

This term focuses on domain-specific AI solutions.

Topics include:

Supervised learning

Time series forecasting

Reinforcement learning

Domain constraints

Ethical AI practices

Regulatory compliance

Students design AI systems tailored to specific industries.

Term 6: MLOps, Governance and AI Leadership

The final term focuses on deploying and managing enterprise AI systems.

Topics include:

MLOps pipelines

Continuous Integration and Continuous Deployment (CI/CD)

AI monitoring systems

Responsible AI frameworks

AI system governance

AI leadership and strategy

Students develop enterprise-grade AI solutions ready for production environments.

9. Out-Class Curriculum

The out-class curriculum involves building six real-world AI systems.

Term 1

Applied AI Chatbot Challenge
Students design chatbots that solve real operational problems.

Term 2

Agentic AI Accelerator
Students build autonomous AI agents capable of executing workflows.

Term 3

AI Application in Industry
Students analyze business data using machine learning methods.

Term 4

RAG System Building Challenge
Students create domain-specific AI assistants using Retrieval-Augmented Generation.

Term 5–6

AI Industry Immersion
Students develop AI systems for industries such as:

Retail

Healthcare

Finance

These projects result in market-ready AI solutions.
